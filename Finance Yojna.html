Finance and Financial Security Yojna

Government Schemes

1. Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY)

Launched in 2014, PMJDY is a flagship financial inclusion program that aims to provide every household in India with access to a basic savings bank account, affordable insurance, and pension products. It also offers overdraft facilities and RuPay debit cards, encouraging people to join the formal banking system.

2. Atal Pension Yojana (APY)

APY, launched in 2015, is a pension scheme designed to provide financial security to the unorganized sector. It offers guaranteed minimum pension amounts to subscribers based on their contribution levels and age. This scheme encourages people to save for their retirement years.

3. Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana (PMMY)

PMMY, initiated in 2015, focuses on providing financial support to small and micro-enterprises. It offers loans at different stages, known as Shishu, Kishore, and Tarun, to meet the financial needs of entrepreneurs and help them grow their businesses.

4. Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY)

SSY, launched in 2015, is a savings scheme aimed at securing the financial future of the girl child. Parents or legal guardians can open an account in the name of a girl child and make regular contributions. The scheme offers tax benefits and competitive interest rates.

5. Pradhan Mantri Vaya Vandana Yojana (PMVVY)

PMVVY, launched in 2017, is a pension scheme for senior citizens. It provides a guaranteed pension at a specified rate for ten years. This scheme offers financial security to elderly individuals and is particularly beneficial for those who depend on fixed income during retirement.
